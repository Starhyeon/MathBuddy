Addition Strategies Within 100

NY State Standard: 2.OA.1

üìå Learning Objective

Students will be able to solve one- and two-step word problems involving addition within 100 using strategies based on place value, properties of operations, and the relationship between addition and subtraction.

Understanding Addition Within 100

When adding larger numbers (within 100), we can use several powerful strategies that make calculations easier and faster. These strategies build on what we know about place value!

Strategy 1: Adding by Place Value (Tens and Ones)

We can add the tens and ones separately, then combine them. For 34 + 23: Add tens (30 + 20 = 50), add ones (4 + 3 = 7), then combine (50 + 7 = 57).

Strategy 2: Compensation (Adjust and Compensate)

Make one number easier to work with, then adjust. For 38 + 25: Change 38 to 40 (adding 2), add 40 + 25 = 65, then subtract the 2 we added: 65 - 2 = 63.

Strategy 3: Breaking Apart (Decomposing)

Break one number into friendly parts. For 47 + 28: Break 28 into 20 + 8. Then: 47 + 20 = 67, and 67 + 8 = 75.

Strategy 4: Counting On by Tens, Then Ones

Start with the larger number, count on by tens, then by ones. For 42 + 35: Start at 42, add three tens (52, 62, 72), then add five ones (73, 74, 75, 76, 77).

üìù Worked Example 1: Adding by Place Value

Problem: 45 + 32 = ?

  1. Separate into tens and ones: 45 = 40 + 5, and 32 = 30 + 2
  2. Add the tens: 40 + 30 = 70
  3. Add the ones: 5 + 2 = 7
  4. Combine: 70 + 7 = 77
üí° Visual: (40 + 5) + (30 + 2) = (40 + 30) + (5 + 2) = 70 + 7 = 77

üìù Worked Example 2: Two-Step Word Problem

Problem: Emma has 28 stickers. Her friend gives her 15 more stickers. Then Emma buys another pack with 12 stickers. How many stickers does Emma have now?

  1. Step 1 - First addition: 28 + 15 = ?
    Break apart 15 into 10 + 5: 28 + 10 = 38, then 38 + 5 = 43
  2. Step 2 - Second addition: 43 + 12 = ?
    Break apart 12 into 10 + 2: 43 + 10 = 53, then 53 + 2 = 55
  3. Answer: Emma has 55 stickers in total.

ü§ù Guided Practice

Example 1: Use place value strategy

27 + 41 = ?

Separate: 20 + 7 and 40 + 1. Add tens: 20 + 40 = 60. Add ones: 7 + 1 = 8. Combine: 60 + 8 = 68. Answer: 68

Example 2: One-step word problem

A library has 36 books on one shelf and 48 books on another shelf. How many books in total?

36 + 48 = ? Break 48 into 40 + 8. Then: 36 + 40 = 76, and 76 + 8 = 84. Answer: 84 books

Example 3: Compensation strategy

29 + 34 = ?

Change 29 to 30 (easier!). 30 + 34 = 64. Now subtract the 1 we added: 64 - 1 = 63. Answer: 63
