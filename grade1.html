Addition Strategies Within 20

NY State Standard: 1.OA.6

üìå Learning Objective

Students will be able to add and subtract within 20 using multiple strategies including counting on, making ten, using doubles, and decomposing numbers. Students will demonstrate understanding of how these strategies relate to place value.

Understanding Addition Strategies

There are many ways to add numbers! Learning different strategies helps us solve addition problems faster and understand numbers better. Here are four important strategies:

Strategy 1: Counting On

Counting on means starting with the larger number and counting up. For example, to solve 8 + 3, start at 8 and count: "9, 10, 11." The answer is 11!

Strategy 2: Making Ten

Making ten uses the fact that 10 is an easy number to work with. We break apart numbers to make 10 first, then add what's left. For 7 + 5, think: "7 needs 3 to make 10, so I take 3 from the 5. That's 10 + 2 = 12!"

Strategy 3: Using Doubles

Doubles are easy to remember (like 6 + 6 = 12). We can use doubles to solve near-doubles. For 6 + 7, think: "I know 6 + 6 = 12, and 7 is one more than 6, so 6 + 7 = 13!"

Strategy 4: Breaking Apart Numbers

We can break apart numbers into tens and ones to make addition easier. For 13 + 5, think: "13 is 10 + 3. So 10 + 3 + 5 = 10 + 8 = 18!"

üìù Worked Example 1: Making Ten Strategy

Problem: 8 + 5 = ?

  1. Start with 8: We want to make 10. How many more does 8 need to make 10? It needs 2.
  2. Break apart 5: We can break 5 into 2 + 3. Now we have: 8 + 2 + 3
  3. Make ten first: 8 + 2 = 10
  4. Add what's left: 10 + 3 = 13
üí° Visual: 8 + 5 ‚Üí 8 + (2 + 3) ‚Üí (8 + 2) + 3 ‚Üí 10 + 3 = 13

üìù Worked Example 2: Using Doubles

Problem: 7 + 8 = ?

  1. Notice they're close: 7 and 8 are very close numbers (only 1 apart)
  2. Use a double: I know that 7 + 7 = 14
  3. Adjust: But we have 8, not another 7. That's 1 more than 7.
  4. Add the extra: 14 + 1 = 15
üí° Remember: 7 + 8 is the same as "double 7, plus 1 more"

ü§ù Guided Practice

Example 1: Use the Counting On strategy

9 + 4 = ?

Start at 9 (the larger number), then count up 4: "10, 11, 12, 13." Answer: 9 + 4 = 13

Example 2: Use the Making Ten strategy

6 + 7 = ?

6 needs 4 to make 10. Break 7 into 4 + 3. So: 6 + 4 + 3 ‚Üí 10 + 3 = 13. Answer: 6 + 7 = 13

Example 3: Use Doubles

5 + 6 = ?

I know 5 + 5 = 10. Since 6 is one more than 5, I add 1 more: 10 + 1 = 11. Answer: 5 + 6 = 11

Example 4: Breaking Apart

14 + 3 = ?

Break 14 into 10 + 4. Then: 10 + 4 + 3 = 10 + 7 = 17. Answer: 14 + 3 = 17
